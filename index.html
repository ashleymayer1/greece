What You're Getting

⛵ The Charter

  • Private Lagoon catamaran — 5 cabins, 4 bathrooms, just the 6 of us
  • Professional skipper navigates and handles all sailing
  • Onboard cook prepares breakfast and lunch daily; group eats dinner ashore each evening
  • Island-hopping in the Aegean: Argosaronic islands (Hydra, Poros, Spetses, Aegina) or Cyclades depending on conditions
  • Route finalized by captain ~1 week before based on weather — we can share island preferences ahead of time
  • Water toys: snorkel gear, stand-up paddleboard, underwater scooter, large tube, motorized tender

🏛️ Athens Pre-Trip

  • Arrive May 13 — full recovery day after overnight flight, check in, settle in
  • Full day May 14: Acropolis & Parthenon, Acropolis Museum, Plaka neighborhood, lunch and dinner
  • Board the catamaran May 15 at 5pm from Nea Peramos marina (~45 min from central Athens)
  • Hotel: targeting a Marriott-brand property in central Athens, 2 per room

✈️ The Flights

  • RDU → ATH with one connection (likely JFK or ATL on Delta)
  • Depart evening May 12, arrive Athens morning May 13 after an overnight transatlantic leg
  • Return evening May 20 from ATH, arrive RDU late night
  • No visa required for US citizens — EU biometric entry (EES) required at border on first entry
  • Passports must be valid through at least November 2027

💰 What the APA Covers vs. What It Doesn't

  • Covers: fuel for the charter route, all marina and port fees, onboard food and drinks
  • Crew food is included separately — not from the APA
  • Does not cover: dinners in port town restaurants, extra alcohol, paid shore excursions
  • Unspent APA is refunded in cash at disembarkation
  • If the group spends over the APA estimate, the difference is settled with the captain before leaving

Don't Forget These

💳 Cash & Currency Greece uses euros. Bring cash for the boat — the APA refund is in cash, crew tip is cash. ATMs are widely available in Athens and port towns. Notify your bank before you travel.
🔌 Power Adapters Greece uses Type C/F outlets (two round pins, 230V). US devices need a plug adapter. Most modern chargers handle 110-240V, but check hair tools and high-wattage items.
📱 International Phone Plan Add a plan before you fly. WiFi is on the boat, but you'll want data in Athens and in port. Most US carriers offer international day passes or monthly add-ons.
🛂 Passports Must be valid through at least November 20, 2027. Renew now if needed — processing has had long backlogs.
🧳 Pack for a Boat Cabin space is limited. Soft-sided bags (duffels), not hard-shell suitcases. Layers for evenings, swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen.
🌊 Sea Sickness The Aegean in mid-May is generally calm and catamarans are stable — but bring Dramamine if you're prone to motion sickness.
💡 Insurance Timing CFAR coverage must be purchased within 10–14 days of your first deposit. Group policies covering all 6 travelers are available — compare at InsureMyTrip.com.
